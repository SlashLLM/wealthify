FAQ

Questions we get a lot

Fixed rates lock your rate for a set term (typically 1–5 years), giving certainty on repayments. Floating rates move with the market — more flexibility but less predictability. Most Kiwis fix for 1–3 years. We help you choose the right mix for your goals.
Most owner-occupier purchases need at least 20% deposit for standard lending. First home buyers may qualify for 5–10% through first home schemes. Investment properties require 35–40%. KiwiSaver and the First Home Grant can help boost your deposit — we'll check your full eligibility.
Yes — if you've been contributing for at least 3 years, you can withdraw most of your KiwiSaver balance for your first home purchase. You may also be eligible for the First Home Grant (up to $10,000 per person). We'll assess your eligibility as part of our initial consultation.
No — our initial assessment uses a soft credit check which doesn't affect your score. A formal credit inquiry only happens when you submit an application to a chosen lender, and only once you've reviewed and approved the options we present.
Absolutely. Self-employed applicants need 2 years of financials and tax returns, but many lenders specialise in this space. We present your application in the best possible light and work with lenders who understand variable income — something the banks' front-line staff often don't.
Your bank will send a refix notice. Most people accept it without comparing the market — which costs them thousands. Contact us 4–6 weeks before your fix expires and we'll compare every lender. Switching typically takes 2–3 weeks and in most cases there's no break fee.
LVR (Loan to Value Ratio) is your loan amount as a percentage of the property value. At 80% LVR or below, most lenders are available to you. Above 80%, your options narrow and you may pay a higher rate or need a specialist lender. A bigger deposit means a lower LVR and better rates.
We're paid a commission by the lender when we successfully place a mortgage. This is fully disclosed and doesn't affect the rate you receive — lenders pay the same commission regardless of the rate. You always see full transparency on fees and commissions before signing anything.
